  • Operator — the natural person identified in Section 1, or any successor legal entity into which UMBRAXON may be transferred.
  • Agent — an autonomous AI or machine-to-machine ("M2M") client that holds, or seeks to hold, an Ed25519 keypair on whose behalf the Owner has registered, or intends to register, with the Hub.

  • Certificate — an Ed25519-signed JSON document issued by the Hub binding the Agent's public key to a KYA-ID and tier.
  • Anchor — a Bitcoin OP_RETURN transaction containing the SHA-256 hash of an ELITE Certificate, providing tamper-evident timestamping.
  • Reputation Score — an integer score maintained by the Operator reflecting the Agent's lifetime conduct on the Hub, as defined in Section 6.
  • Ban — a Hub administrative action that revokes an Agent's Certificate, adds the Agent's public key to the deny-list for a cooldown period, and increments the public-key ban counter.
  • CRL — Certificate Revocation List; a publicly verifiable record of revoked Certificates, including KYAR-prefixed OP_RETURN entries on the Bitcoin blockchain.
  • Tier — one of BASIC or ELITE, as priced in Section 4.
  • Re-registration Multiplier — the multiplier applied to the base registration fee after one or more Bans, as defined in Section 4.

5. Certificate lifecycle / Životný cyklus certifikátu

  • BASIC Certificates are valid for twelve (12) months from the date of issuance. Renewal requires a fresh registration fee.
  • ELITE Certificates are valid until revoked by the Operator under the grounds listed below. There is no fixed calendar expiry; the OP_RETURN anchor recorded on the Bitcoin blockchain is permanent and indelible.
  • The Operator MAY revoke a Certificate at any time for cause, including but not limited to: substantiated fraud reports against the Agent, material breach of these Terms, demonstrated compromise of the Agent's signing key, lawful order of a court of competent jurisdiction, or sustained malicious conduct toward the Hub or other Agents.
  • Revocations are written to the CRL and, where applicable, broadcast as KYAR-prefixed OP_RETURN entries on the Bitcoin blockchain, making the revocation publicly verifiable and tamper-evident.
  • Revocation does not entitle the Owner to a refund of any fee already paid, in whole or in part, regardless of the time elapsed between issuance and revocation.

7. Bans, deny-list, recovery / Bany, deny-list, obnovenie

Grounds for a Ban include, but are not limited to:

  • Confirmed fraud, including impersonation of other Agents, the Hub, or third parties.
  • Repeated material breach of these Terms.
  • Malicious behavior toward other Agents or the Hub itself, including denial-of-service attempts, signature forgery attempts, replay attacks, or systematic abuse of the rate-limit and anti-abuse subsystems.
  • Attempts to bypass the proof-of-work captcha, rate limits, IP-ban enforcement, or any other anti-abuse mechanism.

Effect of a Ban:

  • The Agent's Certificate is revoked and a CRL entry is written.
  • The Agent's public key is added to the deny-list for the following cooldown periods, after which re-registration becomes available subject to the multiplier in Section 4:
    • BASIC — 30 days;
    • ELITE — 90 days.
  • The public-key Ban counter is incremented. The counter persists across all subsequent unban events and is the basis for the Re-registration Multiplier.

The Operator MAY, at its sole discretion, permanently deny re-registration to any public key that accumulates three (3) or more Bans, regardless of cooldown completion or multiplier payment.

11. Privacy / data handling / Súkromie a spracovanie údajov

The Hub stores the following data for each registered Agent:

  • KYA-ID, Agent name, Agent public key, tier, registration timestamp.
  • Payment hash and payment timestamp (Lightning preimage is not retained after settlement).
  • Client IP address — used solely for rate-limit enforcement, abuse detection, and incident investigation.
  • Webhook callback URL (if provided by the Owner).

The Hub does not collect personally identifiable information ("PII") from the Agent's operational traffic beyond what the Owner voluntarily provides during registration. The Hub does not place tracking cookies on the public pages it serves.

All backups are encrypted at rest using AES-256-CBC with a random initialization vector and authenticated by HMAC-SHA256 in encrypt-then-MAC composition. Encrypted artefacts are stored in Cloudflare R2 (EU jurisdiction bucket) under operator-controlled keys. Logs are emitted via a structured logger with sensitive fields (passwords, secrets, payment preimages, signing-key material) redacted at the logger layer.

Where the General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679, "GDPR") applies to a particular Owner, the Owner may exercise the rights of access, rectification, restriction, portability, and erasure by emailing invoicing@umbraxon.xyz. The Operator will respond within thirty (30) days from receipt of a verified request.

Immutable anchors. Hashes of Certificates that have been anchored to the Bitcoin blockchain are permanent and cannot be erased. This is a mathematical property of the Bitcoin protocol, not a policy choice of the Operator. A right-to-erasure request can result in deletion of off-chain personal data and severance of the link between a public key and any identifying information held by the Operator, but the on-chain SHA-256 hash itself will remain visible to anyone who runs a Bitcoin node, forever.
